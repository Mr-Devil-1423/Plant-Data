Growth Analysis of Pithecellobium dulce (Manila Tamarind Sapling): A Field Observation Report (May–June 2025)

Name of the Plant

  • Common name: Kodikkaai chedi / Kodikkaai maram
  • English name: Manila Tamarind
  • Other names: Madras Thorn, Sweet Inga
  • Scientific name: Pithecellobium dulce

Type of Plant

Large tree species (sapling stage: small shrub-like plant)

Growth Period Observed

Observation period: May 1, 2025 to June 21, 2025 (7 weeks approx)

Growth Conditions

  • Soil: Well-drained loamy soil mixed with organic waste (vegetable peels, eggshells, milk residue, tea powder)
  • Watering: Moderate watering every 2–3 days, keeping soil moist but not waterlogged
  • Sunlight: Full sunlight (typical of Tamil Nadu weather)
  • Temperature: Warm tropical climate, around 30–35°C

Special Care Taken

  • No chemical pesticides used
  • Natural compost and organic kitchen waste used as fertilizer
  • Plant kept in open air for better sunlight and airflow

Additional Notes

  • Seed germination usually occurs within 7 days
  • Growth rate about 2–3 cm per week in ideal conditions
  • Leaves start to develop clearly by the 2nd week
  • Sapling becomes sturdy enough for transplanting after 6–7 weeks

Botanical Description

Pithecellobium dulce is a thorny, medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas but widely naturalized in India and other tropical countries. It typically grows up to 10–20 meters tall, with bipinnate compound leaves, fragrant flowers, and coiled pods containing edible sweet pulp.

Ecological Importance

The Manila Tamarind plays an important role in soil stabilization and acts as a pioneer species in degraded lands. It provides food and shelter to many birds and insects. Additionally, its nitrogen-fixing roots enrich the soil quality, supporting nearby plant growth.

Uses of the Plant

  • Fruit pulp is edible and used in traditional sweets and beverages
  • Leaves and bark have medicinal uses in folk remedies
  • Wood is used for fuel and light construction
  • Ornamental and shade tree in urban landscaping

Conclusion

Observing the growth of the Kodikkaai sapling was both educational and exciting. Each week brought visible progress, from the first sprouting leaves to a sturdy young plant ready for the next stage of life. This project deepened my understanding of plant development and the importance of natural, chemical-free care in gardening.
